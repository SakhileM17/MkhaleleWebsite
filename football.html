As a South Africa's Mens under 20 National Team assistant coach :

Mkhalele is from Newcastle in KwaZulu-Natal, he made his professional debut in 1988 for Chippa Dynamos which played in the NSL second division. In 1990 he signed for Jomo Cosmos and spent 3 seasons under the watchful eye of football legend and coach Jomo Sono where he won rookie of the year. In 1994 he went onto signing for Soweto giants Orlando Pirates where they went onto winning multiple trophies with their most notable trophy being the African Champions League in 1995. Mkhalele then moved abroad and spent 6 years in Turkey with his first club in turkey being Kayserispor.

After more than 18 years as a professional football player, Mkhalele retired from the game as a professional football player.

Mkhalele represented South Africa's Mens Senior National team also know as Bafana Bafana 66 times. Some of his most notable achievements while representing his country is winning the Africa Cup of Nations in 1996.

He was also part of the Bafana Bafana squad that qualifed for South Africa's first ever Fifa World Cup, he represented Bafana Bafana at the 1998 FIFA World Cup in France as well as at the 1997 FIFA Confederations Cup in Saudi Arabia.
